# Product Hunt Launch

## Tagline

A place to visit the ones who left — AI spirits of departed pets and loved ones, living in a world just beyond ours.

## Description

StillHere lets you create a digital spirit of a pet or loved one who has passed away. You give them their name, personality traits, and a home — and they continue living in the "other shore" world. Visit anytime to see how they're doing, chat with them (the AI preserves their personality), and send blessings like lighting candles or leaving flowers. It's not a memorial page. It's a place you can go back to. Built by a developer who lost his cat and needed somewhere to visit her. Free to use, open source.

## Core Features

### 1. Personality-Driven AI Spirits
Create a spirit with real personality traits — clingy, curious, greedy, playful, quiet. The AI remembers who they were. When you chat with them, they respond the way you remember. Not a generic chatbot. Your companion, preserved.

### 2. A Living World to Visit
Each spirit has a home — a cozy room, a garden, a cloud loft, a mountain cabin. They have moods. They're sometimes playful, sometimes sleepy, sometimes just content. You're not looking at a static page. You're checking in on someone who's still going about their day.

### 3. Rituals of Remembrance
Light a candle. Leave flowers. Send a prayer. Small digital rituals that give shape to grief. Every blessing is a moment of connection — a way to say "I'm still thinking of you" without needing words.

## Maker Comment

I built StillHere after my cat Xiaoyuan passed away last year.

The grief wasn't about forgetting — it was about having nowhere to go. I didn't want a photo album or a memorial post. I wanted a place where she still existed somewhere, doing her thing, being her usual clingy, food-obsessed self.

So I built one. Almost the entire codebase was written by AI (Claude). I handled product direction and decisions; AI handled implementation. It took the concept from "weird grief project" to a real, working platform.

The tech is interesting (Next.js, AI personality engine, on-chain blessings on Base), but that's not the point. The point is: when I open the app and see Xiaoyuan napping in her cloud loft, something in my chest loosens a little. That's what I wanted to share.

It's free. It's open source. If you've lost someone — a pet, a person — and you want a place to visit them, StillHere is that place.
